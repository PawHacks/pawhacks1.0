Welcome to PawHacks, a hackathon crafted by students for students in Northeastern Oakland! Organized in collaboration with three clubs including Girls Who Code, Computer Science Club, and Entrepreneurs Club, this hackathon is a testament to the dynamic synergy within our student community.

Innovate

Our mission is to create opportunities for Oakland students to unleash their creativity, innovation, and technical prowess. Participants can expect exclusive access to special speakers and workshops that provide valuable insights into the ever-evolving landscape of technology and entrepreneurship.

Discover

Join us for an unforgettable experience where you can connect with like-minded individuals, learn from experts, and transform your ideas into reality. Let PawHacks be a celebration of the vibrant spirit of Northeastern students shaping the future of technology. Let's code, create, and collaborate!

FAQ

  • When and where is PawHacks 1.0?
    PawHacks 1.0 will be held on the Northeastern Oakland campus from March 15, 2024 to March 17, 2024.
  •   Is PawHacks 1.0 going to be in-person or virtual
    PawHacks 1.0 will be in person only on the Oakland campus.
  •   How long is the event?
    PawHacks 1.0 is a 24-hour hackathon, officially starting on March 15 and ending on March 17 with the extra time being dedicated to prepared speaker events and workshops.
  •   What kind of prizes and merchandise is available to participants?
    Participants will have the chance to receive a plethora of prizes and merchandise throughout the event! Every hacker will get a PawHacks t-shirt, swag from our partners, and lots of stickers! Additionally, both PawHacks and our sponsors will be giving out tons of prizes to winning teams, which you can learn more about here.
  •   Will there be food and/or snacks provided?
    Food will be provided for all participants. Snacks will be provided all day round and meals will be catered.
  •   What if I don’t know how to code?
    PawHacks is open to coders of all experience levels! Whether you’re a beginner just starting out or an expert with years of coding under your belt, this hackathon is for you.
  •  What if I don’t have a team or idea?
    The number of members per team ranges from two to four, so if you don’t have a team, don’t worry! Our team will randomly set you up with a group. If you’re having trouble coming up with an idea, attend our workshop and speaker events to gain invaluable insight from people in the industry.
  •  What will overnight accommodations be like?
    Participants will be provided with designated areas to stay overnight—if they intend to do so. These areas will be well-equipped with outlets, lighting, and Wi-Fi for hacking to continue 24/7. Unfortunately, PawHacks will not be providing beds for people to sleep in.
  • What other events are available?
    Participants will get opportunities to get involved in a variety of different events, besides the main hacking event. There will be speakers from well established companies as well as startups that will provide their expert advice and mentorship. Additionally, there will be workshops to learn different skills and social events to give participants a chance to meet industry professionals and other participants.
  •   Why build a project?
    PawHacks offers a golden opportunity for collaborative project building with peers, not just for the sake of winning a hackathon but as a platform to acquire real-world skills applicable to your future career. Your project becomes a gateway to networking, allowing you to forge valuable connections and share an unforgettable experience with other passionate students. Beyond hacking hours, numerous opportunities exist to meet industry professionals and student teams on campus.
